Bulz

Description

Bulz is a specific Romanian dish, prized by shepherds, made of well-boiled mămăligă and cheese or urdă. A large piece of the mămăligă is placed on a plate, a wooden spoon is used to make a hollow, which is then filled with cheese

Ingredients

  • 500 g malai
  • 1 litre milk
  • 300 g kaiser
  • 500 g butter cheese
  • 8 eggs
  • 50 g butter
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 200 g sheep's milk cheese for garnish
  • 150 g bacon
  • 2-3 slices of green onion for garnish

How to prepare (Let's cook!)

  1. Cut the kaiser into strips and cook with 2 tablespoons of oil. Brown a little then turn off the heat and set aside.
  2. Fry the eggs separately.
  3. In a saucepan or pot, boil the milk with a litre of water. Add a little salt and a bit of malai.
  4. Leave on the heat until it boils and sift in the maize, stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or a large wooden spoon, or with a wooden spoon.
    You must stir quickly to prevent lumps from forming.
  5. After adding the maize, leave on the heat for a few minutes, stirring constantly until it begins to thicken, then turn off the heat.
    Most of the bags of malt we find on the market have specified on the instructions the boiling time, follow the written instructions.
  6. Take 8 yenna, clay or metal bowls according to your preference and evenly distribute a layer of the mamaliga.
    You can also use a normal tray, and then transfer the bubble into wooden pots.
  7. Level with a spoon then evenly distribute the curd cheese, fried kaiser, another layer of bacon, then more curd cheese, fried egg, butter and top with another layer of bacon.
  8. Garnish with bacon, and bake in a preheated oven at 180 degrees for about 15 minutes, then remove and garnish with bacon, green onions and serve warm.
